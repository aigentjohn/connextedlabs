Create /docs/handoff/08-user-stories.md. This document captures the experiential knowledge behind the platform — the human stories that explain why each feature exists and what it means to a real person in a real situation. This knowledge was not captured in documents 01 through 07 because it cannot be read from code. It comes from the intent behind the design decisions.
Write seven user stories. Each one follows this structure: a one-line description of who the person is, a paragraph describing their situation today and the specific frustration they feel, a paragraph describing the same situation on Connexted and what changes, and a final sentence describing what becomes possible that was not possible before.
The seven stories are:
Story 1 — The event organizer with a QR code. Someone who runs in-person networking events. Today attendees arrive as strangers, conversations start from zero, energy peaks during the event and evaporates afterward, and follow-up depends on business cards and hope. On Connexted the event has a URL before it happens. QR codes on the door, the screen, and every name badge give anyone instant access to who is in the room, what they do, and the presentation slides. After the event the circle stays open. The room does not have to empty. Connexted is a digital companion for in-person events — not a substitute for them.
Story 2 — The curator who creates new value. A professional who does not only create original content but who has excellent judgment about what others have created. Today that curatorial expertise has no home — they can share links in a chat or a newsletter but there is no persistent, organized, discoverable place for a curated collection. On Connexted they build a Playlist that sequences their own videos alongside others into one curated learning arc, or a Magazine that collects the best writing on a topic from multiple sites including their own. The curation is itself a creative act. The collection has a URL, lives in a circle, and is discoverable by anyone interested in that topic.
Story 3 — The creator freed from a single host. Someone who publishes on YouTube or writes on Ghost or Substack. Their content is good but their reach is controlled by the platform's algorithm. Comments on YouTube are public and often toxic. On Connexted they create an Episode that links to their YouTube video — the video stays on YouTube, nothing moves — but the conversation happens inside a members-only circle where people know each other, comment honestly, and engage without exposing their contact information to strangers on the internet. Private enough to be honest.
Story 4 — The AI workflow builder. A solopreneur who has built custom GPTs, prompt libraries, or Claude Projects that make their work dramatically more effective. Today there is nowhere to share these that gives them credit, builds their reputation, or lets a community validate and improve them. On Connexted they share the URL of their AI tool inside a Prompt container in a relevant circle. It is tagged by topic, discoverable by other members, and becomes part of their professional presence — proof of expertise in a form that did not exist before.
Story 5 — The invisible professional. Someone in career transition who has been doing real work — contracting, building, learning, creating — for months but whose LinkedIn profile still shows their last job title. Their new work is invisible. On Connexted their profile shows their actual journey — the Build workspaces documenting projects in progress, the badges earned for completing programs, the circles they are active in, the topics they have tagged themselves with. Their presence reflects who they are becoming, not who they were.
Story 6 — The Meetup organizer who owns nothing. Someone who built a community of thousands on Meetup. They do not own a single email address. Meetup owns the member data, controls the communication channel, and captures the relationship. On Connexted the same community exists on a platform they control. The member data is theirs. The circle is theirs. The conversation history is theirs. The relationship does not disappear if they stop paying Meetup.
Story 7 — The startup with a pitch trail. A two or three person founding team that pitches investors every week. Today each pitch is a different file emailed to a different person with no version history, no feedback trail, and no way to know who actually looked at it. On Connexted the pitch lives at one URL inside a Pitch container. Every version is there. Reviewers comment inside a members-only circle so feedback stays private and contextual. The pitch improves visibly over time and the trail of that improvement is itself a signal of seriousness.
After the seven stories add one short section titled The design philosophy behind the stories. Cover three points in plain prose: first, that Connexted organizes around what people are doing — the activity — not what format their content is in, because the URL is the content and the container is the intention. Second, that the platform is a digital companion for in-person events and not a substitute for them — the human moment belongs to the humans, Connexted handles everything before and after. Third, that privacy by design means members-only circles create a space that is private enough to be honest — something no public platform can offer.
No bullet points. No feature lists. Write every story as if you are describing a real person you know. Confirm scope and proceed.
