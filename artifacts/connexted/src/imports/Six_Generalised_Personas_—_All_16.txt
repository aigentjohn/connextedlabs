Six Generalised Personas — All 16 Archetypes Covered
Persona 1: "The Deep Diver"
Core driver profile

Individual: Personal Growth & Learning, Autonomy & Control
Business: Risk Management, Knowledge & Culture Building
Community: Knowledge & Culture Building
Primary archetypes: 🦉 Curious (Owl), 🐙 Cataloguers (Octopus)
Also fits: 🐘 Coaches (Elephant) (learning dimension), 🦎 Careerseekers (Chameleon) (early-stage learning focus)

Who they are: Someone whose innovation energy comes from understanding — researching, capturing, organising, or passing knowledge on. They are the community's intellectual infrastructure. They produce insights, preserve learning, and manage the organisation's understanding of risk. They are often undervalued because their output is invisible until it's missing.

User stories

As a Deep Diver, I want to tag my contributions with my Individual Driver (Personal Growth) so that the community understands why I contribute, not just what I contribute
As a Deep Diver, I want to find other members who share the Knowledge & Culture Building community driver so that my work feeds into something lasting rather than disappearing
As a Deep Diver, I want to see which business problems (Risk Management, Customer Insight) my archetype is most suited to solve so that I can position myself usefully inside organisations
As a Deep Diver, I want a structured way to publish research or knowledge — not just a discussion post — so that depth is preserved and searchable by others
As a Deep Diver, I want to find complementary archetypes (Lions, Eagles) who will act on my findings so that my work moves from insight to impact
Persona 2: "The Maker & Solver"
Core driver profile

Individual: Problem-Solving Satisfaction, Creative Expression
Business: Customer Value Creation, Operational Efficiency
Community: Quality of Life Enhancement
Primary archetypes: 🦫 Catalysts (Beaver), 🕷️ Creators (Spider), 🐜 Contractors (Ant)
Also fits: 🦊 Consultants (Fox) (problem-solving dimension), 🐦 Contrarians (Raven) (problem identification)

Who they are: Energised by making things work. Whether that's building a prototype, designing a concept, or running an airtight operational system — their satisfaction comes from something tangible being produced. They contribute at the execution layer of innovation, and the community serves them best when it helps them find direction, resources, and champions for the work.

User stories

As a Maker & Solver, I want to post a project I'm working on and tag it with both my Individual Driver (Problem-Solving) and Community Driver (Quality of Life) so that mission-aligned collaborators can find it
As a Maker & Solver, I want to identify which archetypes in the community can champion or fund my work so that my skills are not wasted for lack of direction or resource
As a Maker & Solver, I want to share a STAR story about something I've built so that my craft is visible and not just my job title
As a Maker & Solver, I want guidance on working with archetypes that challenge me (Eagles, Squirrels) so that I can engage with strategic and financial partners without friction
As a Maker & Solver, I want to filter the member directory by Business Driver (Customer Value Creation) so that I can find people whose organisational goals align with what I build
Persona 3: "The People Enabler"
Core driver profile

Individual: Social Impact & Purpose
Business: Talent Attraction & Retention, Ecosystem Resilience
Community: Ecosystem Resilience, Social Problem Solving
Primary archetypes: 🐘 Coaches (Elephant), 🐬 Cheerleaders (Dolphin), 🐝 Connectors (Honeybee)
Also fits: 🦁 Champions (Lion) (social impact dimension), 🦎 Careerseekers (Chameleon) (talent side)

Who they are: Their innovation contribution is other people flourishing. They mentor, energise, and connect. Their value to organisations is that teams perform better because of them. Their value to communities is that the ecosystem holds together. The community must actively make their contribution visible — because it is the easiest type of work to take for granted.

User stories

As a People Enabler, I want a visible record of connections I've facilitated, mentoring relationships I've held, and energy I've contributed so that my work is recognised, not assumed
As a People Enabler, I want to see which orientations are underrepresented in the community so that I can deliberately connect people who fill those gaps
As a People Enabler, I want to filter members by Community Driver (Ecosystem Resilience) so that I can find others who share responsibility for community health
As a People Enabler, I want to signal that I am open to mentoring Careerseekers so that people who need guidance can find me without awkward cold outreach
As a People Enabler, I want the community to surface who has been recently active or recently quiet so that I can direct encouragement and energy where it's most needed
Persona 4: "The Strategic Visionary"
Core driver profile

Individual: Recognition & Achievement, Competitive Advantage
Business: Competitive Advantage, Growth & Expansion, Risk Management
Community: Economic Development
Primary archetypes: 🦅 Captains (Eagle), 🦁 Champions (Lion)
Also fits: 🐿️ Capital Investors (Squirrel) (growth & economic development), 🦉 Curious (Owl) (competitive intelligence)

Who they are: Motivated by building something significant and being known for it. They think at the system level — organisations, markets, economies. In the community they need peer-level conversations with others who understand the pressure of high-stakes decisions. They often cannot have these conversations inside their own organisations.

User stories

As a Strategic Visionary, I want a trusted peer space with other Eagles and Lions where I can discuss challenges I can't raise internally, without competitive risk
As a Strategic Visionary, I want to see how my business drivers (Competitive Advantage, Growth) are shared across members so that I can connect with peers navigating similar organisational pressures
As a Strategic Visionary, I want to find Deep Divers (Owls) working on adjacent research so that I can build my strategic intelligence through community rather than always commissioning it
As a Strategic Visionary, I want my Community Driver (Economic Development) visible on my profile so that I am known as an ecosystem contributor, not just an organisational actor
As a Strategic Visionary, I want to see which projects in the community are aligned with Growth & Expansion so that I can attach resource or advocacy where it creates the most leverage
Persona 5: "The Critical Guardian"
Core driver profile

Individual: Problem-Solving Satisfaction, Social Impact & Purpose
Business: Risk Management, Operational Efficiency
Community: Ecosystem Resilience, Social Problem Solving
Primary archetypes: 🐦 Contrarians (Raven), 🐢 Conscience Keepers (Tortoise)
Also fits: 🦉 Curious (Owl) (risk management), 🐜 Contractors (Ant) (operational efficiency)

Who they are: Their role is to ask the uncomfortable question, spot the flaw, or hold the long-term view when everyone else is focused on speed. They are frequently misunderstood — seen as blockers rather than protectors. The community should help others understand why this archetype is essential, and give Critical Guardians a constructive channel for their perspective.

User stories

As a Critical Guardian, I want to be able to respond to community projects or ideas with a structured "risk or ethics perspective" format so that my challenge is seen as a contribution, not an attack
As a Critical Guardian, I want to find projects tagged with Risk Management or Social Problem Solving business drivers so that I can direct my critical lens where it's most valued
As a Critical Guardian, I want my profile to clearly explain why my archetype challenges ideas — and link to examples — so that new members understand my role before assuming I'm being negative
As a Critical Guardian, I want to find Champions and Coaches who can help me frame my concerns constructively so that my input improves outcomes rather than stalling them
As a Critical Guardian, I want the community to surface "decisions being made without a Raven or Tortoise in the room" so that I can contribute before the damage is done
Persona 6: "The Growth Amplifier"
Core driver profile

Individual: Recognition & Achievement, Autonomy & Control, Creative Expression
Business: Growth & Expansion, Talent Attraction & Retention
Community: Economic Development, Knowledge & Culture Building
Primary archetypes: 🐿️ Capital Investors (Squirrel), 🦚 Content Influencers (Peacock), 🦊 Consultants (Fox), 🦎 Careerseekers (Chameleon)
Also fits: 🐝 Connectors (Honeybee) (economic development), 🦁 Champions (Lion) (recognition)

Who they are: Motivated by scale — of ideas, audiences, resources, or their own careers. The broadest persona because Growth Orientation covers very different people: the investor managing resources, the content creator building audiences, the consultant offering expertise across contexts, and the career-builder using the community as a development platform. What they share is that they measure success by expansion — of reach, impact, capital, or capability.

User stories

As a Growth Amplifier, I want to see a "triple alignment" view showing where my Individual, Business, and Community drivers overlap so that I can find the highest-leverage opportunities to contribute
As a Growth Amplifier, I want to post content (articles, insights, case studies) and have it attributed to my archetype and drivers so that my thought leadership is visible and purposeful
As a Growth Amplifier, I want to find projects at an early stage that match my growth focus so that I can add resource, reach, or expertise at a point where it makes the most difference
As a Growth Amplifier, I want a development pathway that shows how I can build secondary archetype capabilities over time so that I grow beyond my current strengths
As a Growth Amplifier, I want to connect with People Enablers (Elephants, Honeybees) who can open doors for me so that my ambition is supported by strong relationships rather than cold networking
