# Welcome to Connexted

You have probably spent time building things that are scattered across platforms you do not fully own. A course here. A community there. A portfolio on a website that no one finds. An AI tool that lives inside ChatGPT with no way to show it to the people who would value it. A LinkedIn profile that describes who you were, not what you are doing right now.

Connexted is a professional home. Not another tool to learn. Not another platform to migrate your content into. A structured context that makes what you are already doing visible, discoverable, and coherent to the people who need to understand it.

This guide explains how the platform works and what to do first.

---

## The one idea worth understanding before you do anything else

**The URL is the content. The container is the intention. The community is the context.**

Connexted does not ask you to upload files, duplicate your work, or rebuild what already exists elsewhere. Your YouTube video stays on YouTube. Your blog stays on Ghost or Substack. Your AI tools stay in ChatGPT. What Connexted gives each of those things is a second context — a professional frame, a relevant community, and a permanent place in your discoverable presence — without touching the original.

The containers on this platform do not care what format your content is in. They care what you are doing with it. A **Pitch** invites review and feedback. A **Sprint** commits to time-boxed work. A **Build** documents something being made. A **Library** curates resources worth returning to. Whether the content inside any of these containers is a slide deck, a video, a document, a Claude Project URL, or a custom GPT is completely irrelevant — because the container defines the intention, not the file type.

## What to do first

### 1. Complete your profile

Your profile on Connexted is not a resume. It is a message you are sending to the people who might hire you, collaborate with you, or want to learn from you. A good profile on this platform shows what you are doing right now — not just where you have been.

Start with:
- A **headline** that positions you in one line (not your job title — what you actually do)
- A **bio** that describes your work and your approach, not your credentials
- A **"Looking For"** field that tells the right people exactly what you need — a co-founder, a collaborator, feedback, clients, or ideas
- Your **skills and topics** — these are how people find you through search and how the platform surfaces you to relevant circles and programs
- **Portfolio links** — link your existing work from wherever it lives; nothing moves

The platform shows you a completion score and tells you exactly what to add next. A complete profile makes you findable in ways a LinkedIn profile cannot — because the information is structured, topic-tagged, and visible to the platform's matching engine, not just to whoever happens to search your name.

### 2. Join a circle that matches where you are right now

Circles are the community layer of Connexted. They are members-only spaces — which means the people inside them know who else is in the room. That changes the quality of every exchange. Feedback is more honest. Conversation is deeper. People are not performing for the public internet.

Browse circles by **topic** — the curated taxonomy that organizes the platform around who you are and what you are working on. Find one or two that match your current work, your transition, or the community you want to be part of. If a circle requires an application or invitation, that is not a barrier — it is a quality signal. The communities that matter are selective because selectivity is what makes them worth belonging to.

### 3. Create something

The fastest way to become visible on Connexted is not to post. It is to create a container that shows what you are doing.

If you are building something, create a **Build** and document it in progress. It does not have to be finished — the fact that it is visibly being developed is more interesting than a polished case study. If you have an AI tool, a prompt library, or a methodology worth sharing, put it in a **Library** or a **Prompt** container in a relevant circle. If you are pitching to investors, clients, or collaborators, put your deck in a **Pitch** container so feedback arrives in one place and version history is automatic.

What you create becomes part of your indexed, discoverable presence on the platform. Another member looking for someone who does what you do can find you not by reading your resume but by seeing what you have actually done.

## The containers — what each one is for

Connexted has thirteen collaborative container types. Here is what each one is designed to do.

**Build** — Document work in progress. A Build is a workspace for showing what you are making while you make it. It is more honest and more interesting than a case study, because it shows the thinking, not just the result. It is also how you get found by people who are interested in the same problem you are solving.

**Pitch** — Organize a pitch for review. Every version of your deck or proposal lives in one place. Feedback from reviewers attaches to the specific version they reviewed. When you update the pitch, the new version appears in the same container. Investors and advisors who revisit it see not just the current version but the history of how it has evolved — which is itself a signal about how seriously you take feedback.

**Sprint** — Commit to time-boxed work. A Sprint is a structured, time-bounded container for a specific piece of work with a clear goal. It creates accountability without requiring synchronous meetings or a project management tool nobody maintains after week two.

**Standup** — Async accountability. A Standup is where a team checks in on progress without scheduling a call. It creates a running record of what each person is working on and where they are stuck — which is more useful than a status update that lives in someone's inbox.

**Prompt** — Invite a response. A Prompt is a question or provocation designed to generate substantive replies from the people in a circle. It is how a circle leader starts a conversation that a feed post cannot sustain — because the people responding know each other and the context is bounded.

**Elevator** — A short, structured introduction. An Elevator is a brief, formatted presentation of who you are, what you do, and what you are looking for — designed to be shared in community contexts where a longer bio would be too much.

**Meetup** — Organize people around a moment. A Meetup is the container that gives an in-person or virtual gathering a before, a during, and an after. Before the event, the URL gives attendees context about who will be in the room and what to expect. After the event, the circle stays open so the conversation continues somewhere instead of ending in the parking lot.

**Table** — An ongoing collaborative space. A Table is a forum-style container for a recurring conversation, a shared workspace, or a structured discussion that has no defined end date.

**Checklist** — A structured task list with completion tracking. A Checklist is how you turn a process or methodology into something assignable and trackable inside a program module.

**Library (Shelf)** — Curate resources worth returning to. A Library is a collection of links, tools, documents, and references organized around a theme. The person who curates a good Library is exercising expertise — knowing what is worth reading, in what order, and why is a professional skill, and Connexted is one of the few platforms where that skill has a native format.

**Playlist** — Sequence content into a learning arc. A Playlist organizes your own work alongside work from other creators into a single curated path. A Playlist can appear inside a Program module as assigned content, making it a lightweight way to design a learning experience without building a full course.

**Deck** — Slide-based content for teaching or sharing. A Deck is a card-by-card format for presenting ideas, teaching concepts, or sharing a structured argument. It can live standalone or be embedded inside a course or program module.

**Magazine** — A curated publication. A Magazine collects episodes, articles, and posts from multiple sources — your own content and others' — into one publication that reflects your editorial judgment rather than any single platform's calendar.

## Learning structures — Courses, Programs, and Pathways

**Courses** are self-paced learning experiences organized into modules called Journeys. Each Journey contains content items — documents, videos, books, decks, checklists, and more. You track your own progress, pick up where you left off, and mark items complete as you go. Courses can be free, paid, or restricted to members of a specific subscription tier.

**Programs** are cohort-based learning experiences with a community layer built in. They have the same structured module format as Courses, but add a discussion forum, a member directory, calendar sessions, and a participant funnel — from invited through applied, approved, enrolled, and completed. If you are running a coaching program, an accelerator, or a professional development cohort, a Program is the structure it belongs in.

**Pathways** are ordered sequences of Courses and Programs that represent a complete growth arc toward a credential or badge. A learner who completes a Pathway earns a badge that appears on their public profile — not just as a downloaded certificate, but as a visible record of the work that earned it, linked to the portfolio and profile that shows the context behind it.

## Topics and Tags — how you get found

**Topics** are the curated taxonomy that classifies content by audience, purpose, and theme. They carry semantic weight that free-form hashtags do not. When you tag your profile, your circles, and your containers with relevant topics, you become findable by other members browsing those topics and by the platform's matching engine.

**Tags** are user-generated and specific. Where topics are broad (coaching, brand strategy, AI tools), tags are granular (executive presence coaching, prompt engineering for consultants, AI-assisted brand research). Together they let you be found at the right level of specificity for each searcher.

Tag your profile. Tag your content. The platform works for you passively — surfacing you to people who are looking for what you do — but only if it has the structured signals it needs to do that work.

## Community guidelines

Connexted is not a public platform. It is a professional community. The quality of what happens here depends entirely on the quality of what members bring to it.

**Be specific.** Generic engagement — a reaction, a one-line reply, a post that could have been written without reading what it responds to — adds nothing. Specific, substantive responses are the currency of a community worth belonging to.

**Be honest.** The platform is private enough for honesty to be safe. Use that. Feedback given in a members-only circle does not have to perform for the public internet. It can be direct, nuanced, and genuinely useful.

**Curate, don't just create.** The members who create value on this platform are not only the ones who publish original content. The ones who curate well — who know what is worth sharing, in what context, and why — are exercising expertise just as much as the ones who write.

**Protect the room.** The reason a members-only circle creates better conversation than a public comment section is that the audience is known and bounded. Do not undermine that by screenshotting, sharing, or broadcasting what happens inside. The room is good because it is a room.
