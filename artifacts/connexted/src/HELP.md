## Getting Started

### How do I create an account?
Click "Sign Up" and complete the registration process. Your account becomes active immediately.

### How do I navigate the platform?
- **Top Header** — Browse public content, explore circles, and find programs open for enrollment
- **Sidebar** — Access your personal content, joined programs, and enrolled courses
- **Search** — Find content, people, and containers by keyword, topic, or tag
- **Notifications** — Activity in your circles and programs that is relevant to you

### What should I do first?
Three things, in order: complete your profile, find one circle in a topic that matches your work, and create one container that shows what you are doing. The Welcome Guide walks through each of these in detail.

### Why can I not see certain content?
Connexted has four visibility levels. See [Visibility and Access](#visibility-and-access) for the full breakdown.

## Profile and Identity

### What is a profile on Connexted?

A profile on Connexted is not a resume. It is a live record of what you are doing right now — your headline, your bio, the work you have linked, the circles you are active in, the topics you have tagged yourself with, and the badges you have earned. A visitor who lands on your profile should be able to understand your current work, your methodology, and your professional direction without asking you to explain it.

### How do I build a strong profile?

The platform shows a **completion score** that tells you exactly how much stronger your discoverability gets with each addition. The highest-impact items are:

- **Headline** — One line that positions you by what you do, not your job title
- **Bio** — Your work, your approach, and what makes your practice distinctive
- **Topics** — The curated categories that classify who you are (up to 3)
- **Skills** — Specific competencies with self-reported proficiency levels
- **"Looking For"** — What you are currently seeking: a co-founder, collaborators, clients, feedback, a job, advisors
- **Portfolio links** — Your existing work, wherever it lives. Nothing moves. The link is the content
- **Moments** — Short posts documenting what you are working on, thinking about, or learning right now

### How do I edit my profile?
Click your avatar, select "Profile," and click "Edit Profile."

### Can I import my existing profile information?
Yes. The platform supports import via **JSON Resume** format and **vCard** for contact information. Go to Profile > Settings > Import.

### Can I export my profile?
Yes. You can export your full account — profile, content, enrollments — as a JSON document. Go to Settings > Account > Export.

## Circles

### What is a Circle?

A Circle is a community space you belong to. It has a feed, a forum, a member directory, events, and containers — all in one place. The critical distinction from public social platforms is that Circles are members-only by default. The people inside know who else is in the room. They are not performing for the public internet. That is not a limitation — it is the structural condition that makes professional community worth belonging to. The reason public comment sections are shallow is the same reason members-only circles are not: the audience is unknown and the incentives reward performance over substance.

### What are the Circle visibility settings?

- **Public** — Visible to everyone and joinable without approval
- **Members-Only** — Visible and described publicly, but content is only accessible after joining
- **Invite-Only** — Only visible to invited members; prospective members must be invited by an admin
- **Unlisted** — Not searchable or browsable; accessible only via direct URL

### How do I join a Circle?

Browse circles from the Explore page or search by topic. Click "Request to Join" on invite-only circles, or "Join" on public ones. Some circles require an application.

### How do I create a Circle?

Navigate to Circles and click "Create Circle." You will set the name, description, topic tags, and visibility. Invite your first members directly, or share the circle URL for public/unlisted circles.

### Can a Circle be linked to a Program?

Yes. Each module (Journey) within a Program can have its own linked Circle, so the community discussion happening in a given week of a cohort is anchored to what participants are actually working on that week. This is one of the structural differences between Connexted and platforms where community is a separate space from learning.

### How do I moderate a Circle?

As a Circle admin, you can pin posts, remove members, moderate content, manage join requests, and set the discussion rules. Moderation tools are accessible from the Circle settings menu.

## Courses

### What is a Course?

A Course is a self-paced learning experience organized into modules called **Journeys**. Each Journey contains content items — documents, video lessons, audio, books, decks, checklists, and more. You track your own progress, mark items complete as you go, and pick up where you left off. Unlike Programs, Courses do not have cohort enrollment or a community layer — they are designed for self-directed learning at the member's own pace.

### What types of content can appear in a Course?

A Course journey item can be a text document, a video lesson, an audio lesson, a quiz, an assignment, a book, a deck, an episode, a playlist, a library, a checklist, or a discussion. It can also be a collaborative container — a Build, a Sprint, a Pitch, a Standup, or an Elevator — so that "doing something" is part of the learning experience, not separate from it.

### How do I enroll in a Course?

Browse available courses from the Courses page. Click the course you want and then "Enroll." Free courses enroll you immediately. Paid courses redirect you to checkout via Kit Commerce. Some courses are members-only and require an active subscription.

### How is my progress tracked?

Progress is calculated from the completion state of individual journey items. When you mark an item complete, the platform updates your progress percentage for that Journey and for the Course overall. A "Mark Complete & Continue" button in the course player marks the current item done, collapses the viewer, and opens the next uncompleted item automatically.

### How do I create a Course?

Navigate to Courses and click "Create Course." You will fill in the course details, set the pricing type (free, paid, or members-only), and then use the Journey Management tool to add modules and content items.

## Programs

### What is a Program?

A Program is a cohort-based learning experience — structured like a Course, with Journeys and content items, but built for a group of people learning together. Programs add a discussion forum, a member directory, calendar sessions, and a participant status system that models how real coaching programs and accelerators actually work: invited → applied → approved → enrolled → completed.

### What is the difference between a Course and a Program?

| | Course | Program |
|---|---|---|
| **Pace** | Self-paced | Cohort-based (leader-set schedule) |
| **Community** | None | Forum, member directory, linked circles |
| **Sessions** | None | Calendar events tied to the program |
| **Progress** | Individual | Tracked and visible to program leaders |
| **Journey locking** | No — any order | Sequential — each module unlocks after the previous |
| **Enrollment** | Binary (enrolled or not) | Status-based funnel (applied → approved → enrolled) |

### How do I find Programs to join?

Go to Programs > Discover. You can filter by topic, status, and enrollment type. Programs that are invite-only or application-based will prompt you for the appropriate next step.

### How do I create a Program?

Navigate to Programs and click "Create Program." You will choose from a set of templates (accelerator, bootcamp, workshop series, etc.) that pre-configure the program structure. Then use the Program Setup Dashboard to add Journeys, content, sessions, and community settings.

### Can I run a paid Program?

Yes. Paid Programs are configured using Kit Commerce. Set the pricing type to "paid" on the program record and configure the associated Kit Commerce product.

## Pathways and Badges

### What is a Pathway?

A Pathway is an ordered sequence of Courses and Programs that represents a complete growth arc toward a credential. It is the structure above individual courses — a defined progression that leads somewhere: a professional certification, a practitioner-level credential, a demonstrated body of work.

### How do I earn a badge?

Badges are earned by completing a Pathway or by being awarded one by a program leader or administrator. When you complete a Pathway, a badge representing that credential appears on your public profile — alongside the actual work that earned it, not just as a downloaded certificate. Prospective clients and collaborators can see the credential and click through to the portfolio page that shows what it represents.

### Why do completion rates improve with Pathways?

Because finishing means something visible. When completion produces a badge that appears on your public profile and is linked to the demonstrated work behind it, the incentive to complete is external and real — not just personal satisfaction. The difference between a credential that lives on your website as a PDF and one that appears on your Connexted profile linked to your actual work is the difference between something you did and something anyone can verify.

### Where do my badges appear?

On your public profile, in the Badges tab. Each badge shows the issuing organization, the Pathway or program that produced it, and a link to the portfolio work associated with it.

## Content Types

### Documents
Text-based articles, lessons, and resources. Supports rich text and markdown. Can embed external content including Google Docs, Notion pages, Figma files, and YouTube videos by URL. Create from the Documents section.

### Books
Multi-chapter guides organized into structured content with a chapter sidebar for navigation. Good for comprehensive methodology documentation or long-form reference material. Create from the Books section.

### Decks
Card-by-card presentation format — slide decks, flashcards, or structured arguments. Can be created manually or imported via JSON (the platform provides an AI prompt template for generating the JSON in ChatGPT or Claude). Create from the Decks section.

### Episodes
Video content linked by URL — YouTube, Vimeo, or Loom. The video stays on the original platform. On Connexted it gains a second context: a members-only discussion, a place in a program module, or a curated spot in a Magazine. Create from the Episodes section.

### Libraries (Shelves)
Curated collections of resources organized around a theme. A Library can contain documents, links, and references from any source. The curation itself is the expertise — knowing what is worth including, in what order, and why is a professional skill with a native format here. Create from the Libraries section.

### Magazines
A curated publication that collects episodes, articles, and posts from multiple sources — yours and others' — into one place that reflects your editorial judgment. Create from the Magazines section.

### Playlists
A sequenced collection of videos and content from any source, curated into a learning arc. The ordering is the creative act. A Playlist can be embedded in a Program module as assigned content. Create from the Playlists section.

## Containers

Containers are the collaborative workspace layer of Connexted. Each one defines a social contract — an intention — not a file format. A container can hold anything with a URL.

### Build
**What it is for:** Documenting work in progress.

A Build is where you show what you are making while you make it. It is honest, visible, and more interesting than a case study — because it shows the thinking, not just the finished result. When you post a Build in a relevant circle, you become findable by people who are working on the same problem. A technical co-founder who posted a Build documenting what he was working on was found three weeks later by the person who became his co-founder. The Build was the proof.

**How to create:** Navigate to Builds > Create Build.

### Pitch
**What it is for:** Organizing a pitch for structured review.

Every version of your deck or proposal lives in one place. Feedback from reviewers attaches to the specific version they reviewed. When you update the pitch, the new version appears in the same container with a note about what changed. An investor who looks at a pitch six weeks after a first meeting can see the current deck and the history of how it evolved — which is itself a signal about how seriously the team takes feedback.

**How to create:** Navigate to Pitches > Create Pitch.

### Sprint
**What it is for:** Time-boxed, committed work with a clear goal.

A Sprint creates accountability without requiring synchronous coordination. Set the time frame, the goal, and the deliverable. Members working in the Sprint can update their progress inside the container. Used inside a Program, a Sprint is how you make "do the work" a structured part of the learning experience rather than an instruction that lives in a document nobody finds again.

**How to create:** Navigate to Sprints > Create Sprint.

### Standup
**What it is for:** Async team accountability.

A Standup is where a small team checks in on progress — what they are working on, what they completed, where they are stuck — without scheduling a call. It creates a running record that is more useful than a status update buried in email. Used inside a Program, a Standup is how a cohort builds visibility into each other's work without consuming synchronous meeting time.

**How to create:** Navigate to Standups > Create Standup.

### Prompt
**What it is for:** Starting a substantive conversation.

A Prompt is a question or provocation designed to generate real responses from the members of a circle. Unlike a feed post — which generates reactions and one-line replies — a Prompt creates a structured space for members to respond with genuine thinking. It is how a circle leader starts a conversation that has depth.

**How to create:** Create from within a Circle's Prompts tab.

### Elevator
**What it is for:** A short, structured professional introduction.

An Elevator is a brief, formatted presentation of who you are, what you do, and what you are looking for — designed to be shared in community and event contexts where a full bio would be too much. It is also how a Program can structure a "introduce yourself" moment into a cohort experience.

**How to create:** Navigate to Elevators > Create Elevator.

### Meetup
**What it is for:** Giving an in-person or virtual event a before, a during, and an after.

A Meetup is the container that organizes people around a shared moment in time. Before the event, the URL gives attendees context — who will be in the room, what the format is, what materials are relevant. During the event, a QR code on a name badge lets people scan each other and see profiles before they introduce themselves. After the event, the circle stays open so the conversation continues instead of evaporating in the parking lot.

**How to create:** Navigate to Meetups > Create Meetup.

### Table
**What it is for:** An ongoing collaborative space with no defined end date.

A Table is a forum-style container for a recurring conversation, a shared workspace, or a structured discussion that continues over time. Unlike a Prompt — which is a single question — a Table is a room that stays open.

**How to create:** Navigate to Tables > Create Table.

### Checklist
**What it is for:** Turning a process into something assignable and trackable.

A Checklist converts a methodology, a onboarding sequence, or a process into a structured task list with completion tracking. When embedded in a Program module, a Checklist is how you make "follow this process" a verifiable part of the learning experience.

**How to create:** Navigate to Checklists > Create Checklist.

## Topics and Tags

### What are Topics?

Topics are the curated taxonomy that classifies content by audience, purpose, and theme. They are structured and carry semantic weight that user-generated hashtags do not. When you tag your profile, circles, and containers with relevant topics, you become findable by members browsing those topics and by the platform's matching engine.

Each piece of content can be linked to up to **3 topics**.

### What are Tags?

Tags are user-generated and specific. Where topics are broad (executive coaching, brand strategy, AI tools), tags are granular (prompt engineering for consultants, plain-language healthcare communication, AI-assisted pitch decks). Together they make you findable at the right level of specificity.

You can apply up to **10 tags** per piece of content.

### What is the difference?

- **Topics** — Who and why. Curated. Limited. Structured. How the platform surfaces you to the right audiences.
- **Tags** — What and how. User-generated. Unlimited. Flexible. How members search for specific expertise.

Popular user-generated tags can be elevated into the topic taxonomy over time — which means the vocabulary of the platform grows with its members.

## Visibility and Access

### What are the four visibility levels?

- **Public** — Visible to everyone, including logged-out visitors. Searchable.
- **Members-Only** — Visible and searchable only to logged-in members.
- **Private** — Visible only to you and people you explicitly share with.
- **Unlisted** — Not searchable or browsable. Accessible only via direct URL.

### What is the difference between visibility and access?

Visibility controls who can see that something exists. Access controls who can open and interact with it. A circle can be publicly visible (anyone can see it exists and read its description) while its content is members-only (only approved members can read the posts inside). These settings are independent.

### How does enrollment work?

The enrollment system tracks twelve acquisition sources and seven ticket types:

| Ticket type | How it happens |
|---|---|
| Paid | Member purchases via Kit Commerce |
| Free | Direct enrollment, no payment required |
| Trial | Time-limited access |
| Scholarship | Admin-granted free access |
| Membership-included | Benefit of an active subscription tier |
| Gifted | Purchased by someone else for the member |
| Promotional | Campaign or referral-based access |

Each enrollment is a ticket with its own status, progress tracking, and (where applicable) referral link with conversion tracking.

## Account and Settings

### How do I change my password?
Settings > Security > Change Password.

### How do I manage notifications?
Settings > Notifications. You can customize what activity generates a notification and how frequently.

### How do I export my account?
Settings > Account > Export. This produces a full JSON export of your profile, content, and enrollment history. You can import this file to another Connexted instance.

### Can I delete my account?
Yes. Settings > Account > Delete Account. This action is permanent and cannot be undone.

## Troubleshooting

### I cannot log in
- Verify your email and password
- Try "Forgot Password" to reset
- Check that your email address is confirmed
- Clear your browser cache and try again

### Content is not loading
- Refresh the page
- Check your internet connection
- Try a different browser
- Clear browser cache and cookies

### I cannot find content I know exists
- Check that the content is published (not in draft)
- Check the visibility setting — members-only content requires you to be logged in
- Verify that topic and tag settings are correct
- Allow a few minutes for newly published content to appear in search

### A feature is not working as expected
- Confirm you are logged in
- Check whether the feature requires a specific role or permission level
- Try refreshing the page
- If the issue persists, reach out to your circle or program admin, or contact platform support

### I cannot upload files
Connexted is a URL-first platform by design. Rather than uploading files, you link to content wherever it already lives — Google Docs, Dropbox, Notion, Figma, YouTube, Loom, or any publicly accessible URL. This means your content stays on the platform you created it on, and Connexted provides the professional context and community around it. If you need to share a file, host it somewhere with a shareable URL and link it from a Document, Library, or container.

## Membership and Billing

### What does a paid membership include?

Paid membership gives you access to paid circles and programs, enhanced collaboration tools, the full profile feature set, and priority support. The specific features included in each tier are displayed on the Pricing page.

### How does billing work?

Connexted uses Kit Commerce for payment processing. Billing is managed through your Kit account after purchase. Contact your platform operator for refund and cancellation questions.

### What happens to my content if I cancel?

Your profile, portfolio, and public contributions remain visible. Your access to members-only circles and paid programs is revoked. Your personal content (documents, builds, libraries, etc.) remains in your account and is accessible when you rejoin.
